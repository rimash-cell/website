04 - Narrative System
Core Narrative Arc
Identity  >  Narrative  >  Space  >  Senses  >  Behavior  >  Atmosphere  >  Memory
Narrative Layers
Layer What It Says
Origin Space is felt before it is seen. I design for that first feeling.
Tension The GCC builds at extraordinary scale - but scale without emotional intelligence creates environments
people pass through, not places they remember.
Belief Luxury is atmosphere, not objects. Behavior is shaped by environment, not signage. Memory is
designed, not hoped for.
Method Every project begins with identity - not materials, not mood boards. Who is this for? What must they
feel?
Proof Each project is told as an experiential case study: the identity, the narrative, the spatial translation, the
sensory outcome, and the memory it created.
Vision Defining what spatial experience design means in the GCC - and building the studio that sets the
standard.
Case Study Framework
1. Identity Origin
Who is this client? What is the core truth of their brand or vision?
2. Narrative Core
What story must live inside this space?
3. Spatial Translation
How does narrative become architecture, material, and sequence?
4. Sensory Specification
What does this space feel, sound, smell, and move like?
5. Experience Outcome
What does a person remember six months after leaving?
