Hind Al Oud

A complete architectural repositioning. Redefining Middle Eastern perfumery into a futuristic, spatially continuous flagship model.

Core Design Solutions

  • Sensory Routing: Discarded linear retail for an immersive, curved "Museum of Emotions."
  • Material Innovation: Custom structural quartz formulation.
  • Geometric Scalability: Engineered organic facades into a modular kit-of-parts for rapid global deployment.
RIMA ALSHOUFI
Hind Al Oud / Concept Narrative
Design Approach

The "Museum of Emotions" Spatial Concept

Spatial Language

The architecture was conceived as a continuous, flowing envelope. Avoiding orthogonal junctions, the floorplan relies entirely on sweeping radii to physically pull the customer into an uninterrupted journey. The concept acts as a museum—the product is completely recessed, demanding the user engage sensually rather than transactionally.

Materiality & Illumination

Sourced bespoke translucent quartz paired with highly polished marble. To support the stark material geometry, museum-grade integrated lighting was detailed to wash the structural curves exclusively, removing ambient ceiling arrays to generate intimate, high-contrast product focal points.

Modular Rollout Engineering

Designing an organic flagship is visually striking; engineering it to scale across 50+ locations is the true architectural achievement. The curved geometries were deconstructed into a standardized 'kit-of-parts.'

This technical logic governed everything from MEP routing in drop ceilings to the slip-match junction specifications, allowing tight contractor tolerances globally.

Yas Mall, Abu Dhabi · Multi-Brand Ecosystem

HOB

Crafting spatial authority. A commanding 1,000+ sqm department store defined by massive architectural volumes.

Core Design Solutions

  • Monumental Scale: The signature green resin entry portal dictates dominance over the surrounding mall atrium.
  • Spatial Governance: Authored rigid internal retail guidelines enforcing material cohesion across all incoming luxury tenant brands.
  • Fixture Logic: Fabricated 100+ bespoke unifying display cases.
RIMA ALSHOUFI
HOB / Design Language
Design Approach

Architectural Hierarchy & Integration

Structural Command

The core difficulty of multi-brand architecture is visual chaos. If tenant brands dictate their own space, the department store loses identity. My approach was to construct an uncompromising, minimalist architectural shell. The massive volumes and repetitive geometric framing lines force internal cohesion, overpowering the disparate languages of jewelry, fashion, and fragrance brands within.

Material Palette

Anchored by a highly polished emerald resin monolith against raw, monolithic stone cladding. The contrast between high-gloss modernism and heavy lithic mass establishes an updated, confident tone for "Emirati Luxury."

Dubai Mall · High-End Hospitality

ANA

Defining modern Arabian hospitality. A zero-logo structural facade blending completely into a multi-sensory interior dining environment.

Core Design Solutions

  • Macro-to-Micro Integration: Designed the overarching floorplan architecture alongside custom tableware, guaranteeing an unbroken narrative.
  • The "No-Logo" Facade: Utilizing purely architectural massing as the primary brand beacon.
  • Cultural Translation: Avoiding literal Arabesque clichés in favor of modern, abstracted tactile space.
RIMA ALSHOUFI
ANA / Total Environment Design
Design Approach

Crafting the Modern Majlīs

Seamless Narrative Space

A high-end dining environment succeeds only if the physical architecture, staff operations, and guest touchpoints feel authored by a single hand. Rather than pasting a brand identity onto a built envelope, I fused them. The architectural surfaces bleed directly into the interior finishes.

Micro-Materiality

The design relied heavily on sensory layers—honed travertine against plush, muted upholstery. I designed bespoke serveware and operational tooling to perfectly mirror the brutalist-yet-warm structural aesthetic, ensuring visual continuity at every scale.
