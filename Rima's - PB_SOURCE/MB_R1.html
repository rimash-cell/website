02 — Positioning

One-Line Positioning

I design how people experience physical space — translating identity and emotion into luxury, cultural, and hospitality environments.

Three Positioning Pillars

Pillar Definition
Spatial Experience Design Designing the full sensory, emotional, and behavioral experience of a physical environment — not its decoration.
Creative Direction for Environments Leading the vision, narrative, and experiential strategy of spaces from concept through execution.
Sensory & Atmospheric Intelligence Designing what a space feels, sounds, and breathes like — the invisible architecture.

Sectors Served

Sector What It Covers
Luxury Flagship retail, luxury brand environments, high-end boutiques
Cultural & Exhibition Museums, cultural institutions, curated exhibition experiences
Hospitality Hotels, F&B, resort environments — full atmosphere design
High-End Residential Private residences designed around how the resident wants to feel
Experiential Events & Artistic Installations Immersive activations, brand events, sensory installations
Corporate & Workplace Environments Headquarters, executive offices, and corporate campuses where spatial identity, culture, and employee experience are designed with the same intentionality applied to luxury environments — not fit-out, but experience-led workplace design

On "Commercial" projects: Corporate & Workplace Environments is the preferred term. It signals a considered, experience-driven approach to office and HQ design — distinct from generic commercial fit-out — and is consistent with the SED methodology.

Extended Positioning

Every environment tells a story — most tell it by accident. Rima Alshoufi designs the deliberate version: spaces where identity becomes atmosphere, where narrative becomes sequence, and where every sensory detail is chosen to shape how people behave, feel, and remember. She brings founder-level creative direction to every engagement, operating as a strategic partner — not a vendor.

04 — Narrative System

Core Narrative Arc

Identity > Narrative > Space > Senses > Behavior > Atmosphere > Memory

Narrative Layers

Layer What It Says
Origin Space is felt before it is seen. I design for that first feeling.
Tension The GCC builds at extraordinary scale — but scale without emotional intelligence creates environments people pass through, not places they remember.
Belief Luxury is atmosphere, not objects. Behavior is shaped by environment, not signage. Memory is designed, not hoped for.
Method Every project begins with identity — not materials, not mood boards. Who is this for? What must they feel?
Proof Each project is told as an experiential case study: the identity, the narrative, the spatial translation, the sensory outcome, and the memory it created.
Vision Defining what spatial experience design means in the GCC — and building the studio that sets the standard.

Case Study Framework

  1. Identity Origin — Who is this client? What is the core truth of their brand or vision?
  2. Narrative Core — What story must live inside this space?
  3. Spatial Translation — How does narrative become architecture, material, and sequence?
  4. Sensory Specification — What does this space feel, sound, smell, and move like?
  5. Experience Outcome — What does a person remember six months after leaving?

05 — Brand Voice

Voice Profile

Quality In Practice
Calm Never rushed. Space between ideas. Confidence without volume.
Premium Elevated, never generic. Every word chosen.
Spatial Language rooted in physical environments — light, weight, texture, silence, atmosphere.
Modern Forward-looking, clean. Not nostalgic, not academic.
Precise Say it once, say it exactly right. Precision over decoration.
Emotionally Grounded Understands what people feel in space — and designs language the same way.

Voice Rules

Always Never
Spatial vocabulary: atmosphere, environment, sensory, spatial identity, narrative, choreography, memory Interior design vocabulary: decor, furnishings, color scheme, renovation, fit-out
Founder-level voice: confident, considered, visionary Portfolio clichés: I am passionate about, I specialize in
Ground "experience" in physical space Generic corporate language: workflow optimization, synergies
Use UX/UI vocabulary only when the work was actually performed (e.g., directing a digital product as part of an end-to-end brand experience project) — not as general positioning language UX/UI as a core positioning term: user experience design, digital touchpoints, wireframe

Signature Language

"I design the emotional DNA of spaces."
"Identity translated into atmosphere."
"Luxury is not what you see — it's what you feel when you walk in."
"A story you can walk into."
"Spaces that shape behavior."
"Atmosphere is a strategy, not a side effect."

06 — Messaging Framework

Core Messages by Audience

Audience The Gap The Promise
Luxury Founders Your space is designed — but does it feel like you? I design the spatial identity of your brand — the version people walk into, feel, and remember.
Cultural Institutions Visitors are informed. They are not moved. I translate cultural narrative into spatial sequence — turning visits into experiences.
Hospitality Developers Your hotel is specified to the millimeter. But the atmosphere was never designed. I design the invisible architecture: the sensory and emotional layer that turns a stay into a memory.
HNW Residents Every surface is premium. But the space doesn't feel like yours. I design sensory environments built entirely around how you want to feel inside your own home.
GCC Market Entry Your brand works globally. But here, it feels imported. I translate your identity into a spatial experience that feels rooted in this place, this culture, this moment.
Corporate Leaders Your headquarters is finished. But it doesn't feel like your company. I design the spatial identity of your workplace — the environment that makes your people feel what your brand stands for.

Messaging Hierarchy

  1. Lead with the gap — Name the thing the client can feel but cannot articulate.
  2. Prove with narrative — Show, through case studies, how spatial experience design solves what decoration cannot.
  3. Close with authority — Working with Rima is not hiring a designer. It is choosing a creative director who will own the experience.

10 — The Spatial Experience Method

IDENTITY > NARRATIVE > SPATIAL CONCEPT > SENSORY SPEC > CHOREOGRAPHY > ATMOSPHERE > MEMORY

Phase Question Output
Identity Who is this for? What is the core truth? Identity & vision brief
Narrative What story must this space tell? Conceptual narrative
Spatial Concept How does narrative become architecture, material, and sequence? Spatial concept & flow
Sensory Spec What does this space feel, sound, smell, and breathe like? Full sensory specification
Spatial Choreography How will people move, pause, discover, and interact? Movement & experience map
Atmosphere What is the ambient emotional field of this space? Atmosphere direction
Memory What will they remember in 10 years? Signature experiential moment

15 — Biography Suite

Long-Form

Website About · Press kit · Conference programmes · Award submissions

Rima Alshoufi designs how people experience physical space — not how it looks, but how it makes them feel.

A Spatial Experience Designer and Creative Director based in Dubai, she translates the identity and emotional truth of a brand into environments that shape how people move, behave, and remember. Her practice spans luxury retail, hospitality, cultural institutions, and corporate environments across the GCC and Europe.

Every project begins with one question: who is this for, and what must they feel? That answer drives every material choice, every sequence, every sensory decision — until the space becomes something people carry with them when they leave.

Over thirteen years, she has directed flagship retail environments, cultural experiences, brand headquarters, and experiential spaces. As Creative Director at one of the UAE's leading luxury organisations, she shaped a multi-brand portfolio — designing flagship environments and sensory innovation spaces, and directing the 360° campaigns, visual merchandising, and brand activations that animated them across the GCC and internationally.

MBA in Luxury Management, Sup de Luxe (Founded by Cartier), Paris · BA in Interior Architecture · Inside LVMH certified · Design Jury Member, Sup de Luxe, 2025.

Bilingual in English and Arabic. Based in Dubai, active across the GCC and Europe.
