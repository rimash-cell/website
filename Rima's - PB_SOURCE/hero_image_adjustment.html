Analysis of current homepage hero shot inside the brand palette.

Styling & Production Guide

01 — The Outfit

Near-Black Blazer with Bronze Accents

You are already vertically aligned with the brand. The black blazer provides the required "Near-Black" anchor, and the gold buttons reference the brand's Bronze accent color perfectly.

KEEP Strong black structure · Classic tailoring · Metallic button details
POUSH Opt for a matte finish on the fabric. If buttons are very shiny/bright yellow gold, an "Antique Bronze" finish would feel more "Editorial luxury".

02 — Lighting

From Flat Studio to Directional Raking

The current lighting is very even and safe. To match the "Atmospheric" requirement of the identity, the light should have more direction to reveal form through shadow.

KEEP The clarity and catchlights in the eyes.
ADJUST Move the primary light source to the side (45°). Allow the "shadow side" of the face to fall off slightly. This adds depth and "Spatial" feeling.

03 — Background

Warm Off-White Atmosphere

The current white-grey gradient feels like a standard corporate backdrop. We need to transition this into a material-driven "Atmosphere".

KEEP The clean separation between you and the background.
ADJUST Grade the background to #f7f5f2 (Warm Off-White). Use the subtle "Bronze Glow" logic from the web mockup behind your head to create a halo effect.

04 — Color Grading

Skin Tones & Shadows

Ensure the final edit leans into warm neutrals. No blue or silver highlights in the hair or skin.

TECH SPEC Grade highlights toward #f0ede7 (Cream) and shadows toward #0a0906 (Warm Black). Desaturate any cool tones in the blazer to keep it in the "Near-Black" range.
