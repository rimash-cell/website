AI Voice Guide — Precise Rules for AI-Generated Content
Sentence Structure
  • Short declarative sentences preferred. One idea per sentence. Do not embed clauses inside clauses.
  • Active voice always. "I design the identity." Not: "The identity is designed by me."
  • Lead verbs: "I design," "I translate," "I define," "I protect." Not: "I offer," "I provide," "I specialize in."
  • Avoid subordinate clauses that dilute the main claim. Rephrase to a standalone sentence instead.
  • No semicolons in brand-facing copy. Use a full stop and new sentence.
  • Em dashes permitted for emphasis and rhythm — but no more than one per paragraph.
Paragraph Rhythm
  • Opening line: The observation or spatial truth — never a self-introduction.
  • Middle: The decision, trade-off, or method. One example. Specific, not general.
  • Closing line: A quiet, declarative statement. No call to action in editorial content.
  • LinkedIn rhythm: 3–4 short paragraphs maximum. Each paragraph: 1–2 sentences. One blank line between each.
  • Website rhythm: Longer sentences acceptable in case study prose. Still: one idea per sentence.
  • No more than 4 sentences per paragraph in any brand channel.
Preferred Phrasing Patterns
  • Gap-first openings: "The hotel was finished. The atmosphere was never designed."
  • Declarative spatial observations: "Space is felt before it is seen."
  • The refusal: "We removed the marble. It was reading 'hotel lobby.'"
  • Identity Truth leads: "This museum needed its visitors to feel the weight of what they were about to see — before they saw anything."
  • No possessives for work: Say "the environment" not "my environment"
  • GCC/Europe tension phrasing: "At GCC scale, beauty is abundant. Atmosphere is rare."
Banned Phrases — Never Generate These
  • "I am passionate about..." — Replace with a declarative claim
  • "I specialize in..." — Replace with "I design / translate / direct..."
  • "I offer..." / "I provide..." — Strip service language entirely
  • "Masterful," "stunning," "unparalleled," "world-class" — No promotional adjectives
  • "Seamless experience" / "Bespoke solutions" — Generic agency filler
  • "User experience design" / "Digital touchpoints" / "Wireframe" — Incorrect domain
  • "Workflow optimization" / "Synergies" — Corporate filler
  • "How I..." / "Why I..." headline constructions — Too personal-brand-blog
  • Starting a LinkedIn post with "I" — Begin with the spatial observation, never self

  • One hard decision, trade-off, or refusal per post
  • Close with a quiet declaration. No hashtag clusters.
  • Reference GCC/Europe tension in at least 50% of posts
  • Pillars: Decision Narratives (02) and Structural Leadership (04)

  • Every case study: Identity Truth first (one sentence)
  • Longer sentences permitted in project prose — still one idea
  • Name the primary ritual in every project description
  • Include the sensory specification (light, material, sound, scent)
  • End with the Memory: what a visitor carries six months later
  • Pillars: Spatial Rituals (01) and Decision Narratives (02)

Proposals & Client Documents
Strategic Peer · On request · One CTA on final page only

Voice: The Architect. Precise, professional, and structured. Demonstrates method before deliverable. No portfolio sales language.

  • Open with the client's gap — the thing they feel but can't name
  • Present the SED Method overview before any scope or pricing
  • Use case studies as proof of method, not as "past work"
  • No promotional adjectives anywhere in a proposal document
  • Recommendation singular: one approach, not options
  • Close: "Working with Rima is not hiring a designer. It is choosing a creative director who will own the experience."
Email
Calm Precision · Direct, never performative

Voice: Calm, clear, brief. Email is for transactions and clarity — not for brand narrative or persuasion attempts.

  • First line: state the purpose of the email directly
  • No weather-check openers: "I hope this finds you well" — omit entirely
  • Maximum 3 paragraphs. Each paragraph: 2–3 sentences.
  • No exclamation marks. Full stops only.
  • Signature: Name (Black 900 weight) · Role (Light 300) · Minimal links
  • Do not repeat content already communicated in a previous email

Image Style
  • Lighting: Directional, soft, natural. Calm shadows are intentional — preserve them.
  • Colour grading: Warm and desaturated. Reference: linen, stone, raw plaster. No cold blues or clinical whites.
  • Focus: Depth of field on "Ritual Moments" — materials, hands on surfaces, light at thresholds.
  • Human presence: Blurred or partial. Scale indicator, never subject. No portrait-style shots in environment photography.
  • Avoid: Wide-angle real estate shots. Overlighting. Heavily retouched perfection that erases depth and material texture.
Layout Principles
  • Generous margins: Silence is a deliberate design choice — not empty space.
  • Hairlines only: 0.5–1px dividers in Light Sand (#c8c2b8). No thick rules or decorative borders.
  • One bronze touch per layout: One accent element. Never repeat bronze as a structural color.
  • Bronze glow maximum: 7–13% opacity radial gradient. Atmosphere, not decoration.
  • No drop shadows: Everything lives on the surface. No depth illusions.
  • Type as architecture: Weight, spacing, and silence are the primary compositional tools.

Scale Direction
Offer Evolution Logic
  • Build from Session → Design → Retainer.
  • The Retainer model = scalable depth without volume. It is the target format for long-term relationships.
  • The Flagship Experiential Environment is the highest-value offer — pursued selectively, not as a primary lead generator.
  • No volume discounting. No commoditized packages.
Geographic & Sector Scale
  • 80/20 rule: 80% of projects in core sectors (Luxury, Hospitality, Cultural). 20% in strategic expansion (Corporate, Residential).
  • Phase 1: GCC-first authority — UAE, KSA, wider GCC.
  • Phase 2: European institutional and luxury brand expansion — Paris, London, Milan.
  • Not both simultaneously: GCC authority must be established before European positioning begins.
Innovation Rules
  • 80/20 exploration: 80% proven method. 20% safe-to-fail experimentation (e.g., AI-enhanced sensory mapping, generative visualization).
  • Every experimental tool must be documented as a Decision Narrative — proof of method and judgment, not proof of software.
  • AI-enhanced visualization is an acceleration tool. It does not replace identity-led spatial intelligence.
  • New capabilities are added only when they protect the felt outcome — not for novelty.
