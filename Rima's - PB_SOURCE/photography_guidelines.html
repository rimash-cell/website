01 — Photography Philosophy

The subject is never the object.
The subject is the feeling.

This brand communicates spatial experience — the intelligence of designing how a person moves through, reacts to, and remembers a place. The photography must carry that same intention.

A chair is not the subject. The quality of light falling across the chair is the subject. A doorway is not the subject. The anticipation of what is beyond it is the subject. Shoot the atmosphere, not the object.

Not how it looks — how it makes them feel.

The Only Question

Before every shot: what emotion does this frame produce? If you cannot answer it in one word, the frame isn't ready.

The Reference

Hiroshi Sugimoto's theatres. Peter Zumthor's atmospheric photographs. Kengo Kuma's material studies. Images that create presence without clutter.

The Standard

If the image could work for any brand, it isn't right. It must be so specific to a sensory moment that it couldn't belong anywhere else.

Portrait Rule

If a person appears, they are inside the space, not in front of it. The environment is the context, never the backdrop.

02 — Mood Reference

Threshold · Entry

The moment before

Doorways, arches, corridors. The anticipation of what lies beyond the frame. Warm raking light from one side.

Material · Texture

Surface intelligence

Stone grain, fabric weight, the reflection of a polished floor. The camera reads material the way the hand does — tactility translated into light.

Spatial · Atmosphere

Environment as character

The empty room that still feels inhabited. Designed negative space. Stillness that implies presence.

Dark · Quiet

Controlled depth

Near-black frames with a single point of warm light. Drama through restraint, not volume. Used sparingly — one in a series.

Light · Directional

Raking and directional

Light that reveals form rather than flattening it. Long shadows, soft gradients, the hour before and after noon.

Portrait · Spatial

Person inside environment

When a person appears: small within the space, or partially framed. The space is the story. The figure is the scale reference.

Do — Shoot This

Directional, raking light

Side-lit, golden hour, overcast with a single break. Light that creates shadow and reveals form. Nothing flat, nothing even.

Generous negative space

At least 40% of the frame is intentional empty space — sky, floor, wall. Silence as composition.

Threshold and entry moments

The point of arrival. Doorways, arches, the edge of a space. The moment before the reveal.

Material at close range

Stone, plaster, fabric, metal — close enough to read texture. Material is the vocabulary of spatial design.

The inhabited empty room

Spaces without people that still feel like they have just been — or are about to be — used. Emotional residue.

Warm, neutral colour grading

Slight warmth in the shadows. Desaturated highlights. Tones that sit in the same palette as the identity: off-white, warm gray, stone.

Don't — Avoid This

Flat, even studio lighting

Removes shadow. Removes depth. The image becomes a technical record, not a spatial experience.

Centred, posed portrait shots

Person front and centre against a backdrop. This positions the brand as personal, not spatial. The work is the subject.

Busy, cluttered compositions

Too many objects, too many focal points. The image should have one weight and one reading — not five competing ones.

Trend-led colour grading

Heavy presets — film grain filters, dark moody Instagram tones, oversaturated warm filters. The palette should be timeless.

Stock photography

Generic interiors, hands-on-coffee-cups, meeting rooms. If the image could belong to any professional services brand, it does not belong here.

Cool or blue-toned grading

The identity has no cool tones. Any image with a blue or silver cast will feel disconnected from the visual system.

03 — Technical Specifications

Format

3:2 · 4:5 · 1:1

3:2 landscape for website hero and banners. 4:5 portrait for Instagram. 1:1 square for grid and thumbnails.

Resolution

Min 2400px

Long edge minimum for web. Print use requires 300dpi. No upscaling — never publish an image that wasn't captured at full quality.

Colour Space

sRGB · Web
Adobe RGB · Print

Export for web in sRGB. Print-ready documents in Adobe RGB. Always embed colour profile on export.

File Format

JPG · WebP

JPG for documents and print. WebP for all web use — smaller file, same visual quality. PNG only for flat graphics with transparency.

05 — Caption System

Text on image: bottom-left only. Never centred, never top. Caption sits in the final 20% of the frame. Always with a subtle dark gradient beneath it.

Caption style: Bronze label in Medium 500 · 9px · 2.5px tracking. Title in Bold or ExtraBold. Off-white or cream — never pure white on dark images.

Overlay rule: If text cannot be read clearly without a gradient, use a gradient. If it can be read without one, do not use one. Overlays should be invisible.

What to caption: Project name · Category · Location · Year. No more. No hashtags, no descriptive copy on the image itself.
