# BRAND PLAYBOOK — RIMA ALSHOUFI
**V1.0 · February 2026**

This playbook serves as the operational manual for the Rima Alshoufi brand. It translates the strategic core into practical rules for design, content, and communication.

---

## 01 — THE STRATEGIC CORE

### Brand Essence
**Emotion-led spatial intelligence.** Sensory precision. Calm authority.
"Not how it looks — how it makes them feel."

### Identity Statement
Rima Alshoufi designs how people experience physical space — shaping identity, emotion, and narrative into luxury, cultural, hospitality, and corporate environments that are felt long after they are left.

### The Narrative Arc
**Identity > Narrative > Space > Senses > Behavior > Atmosphere > Memory**
We don't design for the photo; we design for the memory.

## 02 — VISUAL IDENTITY (OPTION E LIGHT)

### Color Palette
- **Near-Black (#0a0a0a)**: Headlines, deep shadows, and borders.
- **Warm Off-White (#f7f5f2)**: Primary hero/page background. Stone/Plaster reference.
- **Warm White (#faf9f7)**: Body sections and negative space.
- **Bronze (#b87333)**: Accents only. Exactly one touch per layout.
- **Warm Gray (#666666)**: Body text and secondary metadata.

### Typography (Hanken Grotesk)
- **Display Pair**: Black 900 (First Name) + ExtraLight 200 (Last Name).
- **Headings**: Bold 700 / ExtraBold 800.
- **Body**: Light 300.
- **Labels**: Medium 500 (Uppercase, +4px tracking).

### Graphic Rules
- **Margins**: Generous. Silence is a deliberate design choice.
- **Lines**: Hairline rules only (0.5pt - 1pt) in Light Sand.
- **Atmosphere**: Use subtle bronze glows (7-13% opacity) instead of heavy shadows or gradients.

---

## 03 — THE LENS (PHOTOGRAPHY)

### Philosophy: Atmospheric Truth
Photography must capture the *feeling* of a space, not just its dimensions.

### Technical & Grading
- **Lighting**: Directional, soft, natural. High-contrast shadows are welcomed if they feel "calm."
- **Grading**: Warm, desaturated. Remove all cold blues and clinical whites. Reference: Stone, Linen, Wood.
- **Focus**: Strategic depth of field. Use "Ritual Moments" (focus on a hand on a material, or light on a threshold).

### The Do's & Don'ts
- **DO**: Focus on texture and light behavior. Include human scale (blurred or partial).
- **DON'T**: Use wide-angle "real estate" shots. Avoid overly bright/saturated "Stock" aesthetics.

## 04 — THE VOICE & BIOGRAPHIES

### Voice Profile: Calm Authority
- **Spatial Vocabulary**: Sequence, interaction, threshold, ritual, atmosphere, identity truth.
- **Constraint**: No promotional adjectives ("masterful," "passionate"). Use trade-offs instead.

### Signature Taglines
1. "Not how it looks — how it makes them feel." (Master)
2. "Identity translated into atmosphere." (Designer)
3. "Designing the decisions that protect the atmosphere." (Operator/Operator)

## 05 — OPERATIONS & CHANNELS

### Content Pillars
1. **Spatial Rituals**: Interaction sequences and how people move.
2. **Decision Narratives**: Case studies through the lens of trade-offs.
3. **Luxury Codes**: GCC vs Europe cultural nuance.
4. **Structural Leadership**: Decision gates and vision protection.

### Channel Cadence
- **LinkedIn (P1)**: 3x / week. Focus on "The Operator" (Authority).
- **Website (P1)**: Project-based + Quarterly Editorial.
- **Instagram (P2 - Month 4+)**: Visual evidence only. Thresholds and light. Minimal text.

### The "Identity Truth" Rule
Every case study, post, or project reveal *must* start with one sentence on the "Identity Truth"—the core reason the space exists—before any mention of materials or design.
